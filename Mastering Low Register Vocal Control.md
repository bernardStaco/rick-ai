### The Command Block Framework

The most effective way to lock a vocal register is to create a **command block**—a set of bracketed instructions placed at the absolute **top of the lyrics section**. This block must contain three essential elements:

- **Vocal Timbre:** Describes the physical texture of the voice (e.g., raspy, velvety, damaged).
- **Pitch Boundary:** Uses scientific notation to set a mathematical range the AI cannot exit (e.g., C2 to C4).
- **The Blacklist:** Explicitly forbids the AI from using specific styles, such as falsetto or pop tenor.

The source outlines four specific ways to use these boundaries to achieve different atmospheric effects:

- **The Proximity Effect (B1 to B3):** To create an intimate, "whispering" vocal, use sub-bass ranges combined with production tags such as **"close mic," "kill the reverb,"** and **"sing quietly"** to artificially boost bass frequencies.
- **Mechanical Drone (Single Note):** For industrial or cyberpunk styles, you can restrict the AI to a single note (e.g., **"Stay on D2"**). This removes the melody entirely, forcing a robotic, deadpan character.

### Engineering vs. Describing

The overarching theme of the source is that words like "deep" are subjective to an AI, whereas **musical math (like 80 Hz or D2)** is indisputable. By combining these scientific boundaries with **severe negative constraints** in the "exclude styles" box—strictly banning terms like "tenor" or "high pitch"—users can maintain absolute authority over the singer's pitch regardless of how the melody builds.
